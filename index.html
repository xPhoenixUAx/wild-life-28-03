Coverage areas

Common locations for humane wildlife service requests.

Attics and roof systems

Raccoons, squirrels, bats, birds, and rats often exploit soffits, roof returns, dormers, ridge vents, and builder gaps.

Crawl spaces and foundations

Skunks, opossums, snakes, and rodents commonly use low-clearance voids, lattice gaps, and utility penetrations.

Lawns and landscape beds

Moles, groundhogs, skunks, and foxes can trigger burrowing, tunneling, digging, and subsurface disturbance.

Garages and interior edges

Mice, rats, birds, and snakes may move through door sweeps, storage zones, wall chases, and shared utility lines.

FAQ

Answers homeowners often look for before requesting service referrals.

Do wildlife issues always start in the attic?

No. Yard burrows, crawl-space voids, chimney tops, wall cavities, roof vents, and foundation gaps are all common access points.

Are cleanup and exclusion separate from removal?

Often, yes. Homeowners commonly compare animal removal, sealing work, sanitation, and repair recommendations as separate parts of the project.

Is humane exclusion available for birds and bats?

Effective exclusion methods are a central part of many bird and bat service discussions, especially around seasonal activity and protected roosting periods.
