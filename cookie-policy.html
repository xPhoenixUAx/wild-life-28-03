2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help websites function efficiently and provide insights that improve user experience.

Cookies may include:

  • Session cookies: temporary and deleted when you close your browser
  • Persistent cookies: remain on your device until they expire or are deleted
  • First-party cookies: set directly by our website
  • Third-party cookies: set by external services or partners

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the operation of the website and cannot be disabled.

They support core functions such as:

  • Remembering cookie consent preferences
  • Maintaining session integrity
  • Ensuring site security and accessibility

3.2 Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our content so we can improve clarity, usability, and performance.

They may collect anonymized data such as:

  • Page views and navigation paths
  • Time spent on content sections
  • General usage trends

Example tools may include services like Google Analytics.

3.3 Functional Cookies

These cookies support enhanced functionality and user experience.

Examples include:

  • Remembering language or region preferences
  • Temporarily storing form inputs
  • Improving interaction with informational tools and resources

3.4 Marketing Cookies

These cookies help us understand the effectiveness of outreach efforts and present more relevant content or recommendations.

They may be used to:

  • Measure engagement with informational pages
  • Support advertising relevance
  • Enable limited tracking across websites

4. Third-Party Cookies

We may work with third-party services that place cookies on your device, such as analytics providers, advertising platforms, or embedded tools.

These third parties may collect information about your activity across different websites. We do not control how these cookies operate and recommend reviewing their individual privacy policies for more information.

5. How to Manage Cookies

You have the option to accept or decline cookies.

Most browsers accept cookies by default, but you can modify your settings to control or block them.

5.1 Browser Controls

You can manage cookies through your browser settings. Instructions are available for most major browsers, including:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

5.2 Opt-Out Tools

You may opt out of certain third-party tracking through tools such as:

  • Google Analytics Opt-Out
  • Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA)
  • Network Advertising Initiative (NAI)

Note: Disabling cookies may affect how certain parts of the website function and limit access to some features.

6. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature. At this time, there is no consistent standard for responding to DNT signals.

As a result, our website does not currently respond to DNT browser requests.
